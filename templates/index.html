The Rich Heritage of Indian Railways

First passenger train journey from Bombay to Thane - April 16, 1853

From Steam to Speed: 170 Years of Progress

Indian Railways, one of the world's largest railway networks, began its illustrious journey on April 16, 1853, when the first passenger train steamed from Bombay's Bori Bunder to Thane, covering a distance of 34 kilometers. This modest beginning has evolved into a vast network that serves as the lifeline of the nation.

Colonial Roots

Established during British colonial rule, the railways initially connected major port cities to inland regions, facilitating trade and administrative control.

Post-Independence Growth

After 1947, Indian Railways unified into a national network, symbolizing the country's unity and progress, expanding to connect remote regions.

Modern Transformation

Today's Indian Railways features high-speed trains, luxury tourist trains, and advanced technology while preserving its heritage through UNESCO-recognized mountain railways and historic stations.

Iconic Indian Trains

Darjeeling Himalayan Railway

Known as the "Toy Train," this UNESCO World Heritage site began operations in 1881 and still winds through the stunning Himalayan foothills.

Palace on Wheels

Launched in 1982, this luxury tourist train recreates the royal travel experience of India's maharajas with opulent cabins and fine dining.

Shatabdi Express

India's premier intercity trains introduced in 1988, providing high-speed connectivity between major cities with superior passenger comfort.
